The Kharif Season | June - October

The Kharif season relies heavily on the southwest monsoon, providing the rainfall necessary for crops like Paddy (Rice), Maize, Soybean, Groundnut, Cotton ,Jowar (Sorghum) ,Bajra (Pearl millet), Sugarcane, Tur (Arhar/Pigeon pea), Moong & Urad

The Rabi Season | October - March

Rabi crops require a warm start and a cool, dry growing season, often utilizing irrigation from winter rainfall. Some common rabi crops are Wheat, Barley, Oats, Mustard, Rapeseed, Chickpeas (Chana), Peas, Lentils (Masoor), Garlic, Onion, Coriander & Fenugreek.

The Zaid Season | March - June

Zaid is a short season focused on quick-maturing crops, primarily vegetables and fodder, bridging the gap between major seasons. Some major crops were watermelon, muskmelon.
